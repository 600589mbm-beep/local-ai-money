Most local businesses lose leads in the first 5 minutes

Read time: 3 minutes

Here's the uncomfortable truth: the business that responds first usually wins the job. Studies on lead response have shown for years that contacting a lead within the first five minutes dramatically increases the odds of connecting — and waiting even 30 minutes can cut your chances sharply. Most local businesses are nowhere near five minutes. They're at five hours, or five days, or never.

💡 AI Money Move

Set up an instant auto-response on every new lead — web form, missed call, or DM. The moment a lead comes in, AI fires back a friendly message within seconds:

"Thanks for reaching out! This is [Business]. We got your message and we'll have someone with you shortly. In the meantime, what's the best time to call you?"

That single message buys you time, sets expectations, and keeps the lead from shopping your competitor while they wait.

📊 Local Pro Case Study

A small HVAC company was getting leads from their website but checking the inbox twice a day.

  • Before: Average response time ~6 hours. Roughly 1 in 5 web leads booked.
  • After: Instant AI auto-response plus a 7-day follow-up sequence. Response time dropped to under a minute, and booked jobs from the same lead volume rose noticeably — without any extra ad spend.

Same leads. Same ads. Faster follow-up. More money.

🛠️ Tool of the Week

A texting-capable CRM (the kind that can send SMS automatically from your business number). This is the backbone of speed-to-lead — it's what lets you reply in seconds instead of hours, and it keeps every conversation in one place.

✍️ Script of the Week

Hey [Name], thanks for reaching out to [Business]! Quick question so we can help fast — are you looking to get this handled this week, or just gathering info for now?

Short, friendly, and it gets a reply — which restarts the clock in your favor.
